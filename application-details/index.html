Apply to the Science Scholars Program

Step into a world of advanced science learning designed for ambitious students. Join exclusive courses led by top scientists, explore cutting-edge fields like physics, AI, and neuroscience, and collaborate with a global community of future innovators. This is your opportunity to go beyond traditional education and build a strong foundation for a successful scientific future.

The Application Process

The application consists of three components:

  • Student Application Form to be completed by the student
  • Parent/Guardian Recommendation Form to be completed by one of the student’s parents/guardians
  • Teacher/Mentor Recommendation Form to be completed by one of the student’s teachers/mentors

All forms and supporting materials must be submitted online in English. (Parents/guardians and teachers/mentors may use an online translation service to translate their responses into English.) No transcripts or test scores are required. To apply, you must complete and submit the three forms.

All three forms must be submitted in order for an applicant to be considered for admission.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is SSP a free program?

Yes, SSP is offered to accepted students 100% free of charge. Aside from an electronic device and an internet connection, any resources necessary to complete a course are provided to students by WSF.

How can scholars from different parts of the SSP take courses together?

Lectures are recorded specifically for SSP and are taken asynchronously. Professors and expert teaching fellows join live calls on a weekly basis to discuss and expand on course material and to interact with the cohort.

What are the age restrictions for SSP?

While there are no formal age cutoffs for SSP, you must be able to discuss complex math and science topics at a deep level. You must also be able to participate in SSP for at least one year prior to entering college/university. Students who plan to take a gap year after high school are eligible to partake in SSP during their gap year.

How long is a typical course?

A typical course runs 4–8 to weeks. All courses culminate in a live session with the course professor, during which scholars can ask any remaining questions they have.

How can students contact Science Scholars Program?

Please email us at support@sciencescholarsprogram.org.
